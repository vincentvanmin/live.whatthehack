  • #34 Banking & Insurance - KBC

    How Digital Twin Technology can be used to improve KBC processes in banking or insurance? Digital twins act as a bridge between physical and digital worlds by using sensors to collect real-time data about a physical item. This data is then used to create a digital duplicate of the item, allowing it to be understood, analyzed and optimized. As digital twins grow in complexity and move from being digital representations of single items to models of systems of interconnected things, more businesses are seeing the technology as an opportunity to optimize processes in a sophisticated way, integrating Iot, artificial intelligence and machine learning. It enables organizations to test new ideas, uncover problems before they happen, get answers to new questions and monitor items remotely. So how digital twin technology can be used to improve KBC processes in banking and insurance?

    #IoT #artificialintelligence

  • #35 Banking & Insurance - KBC

    All-in-One (#all-in-one)

    Customers expect a more complete solution. For financial institutions it could be very hard to meet the customer needs within the boundaries of their solid closed environment. It could be more challenging for financial institutions to reach new customers, especially in situations where financial products are only enablers. Ecosystems with a total ‘butler-service’ are to be expected in which financial services only play a secondary role.

    What is the relevance for banking & insurance?

    • Client-centricity is key: it could be increasingly difficult for financial institutions to stay relevant for customers. How to make sure that financial institutions are in the lead of ecosystems?

    • Banks could become white-label product factories as they typically provide secondary products. How to avoid becoming the invisible bank?

  • #37 Banking & Insurance - KBC

    Privacy is the New Green (#privacyisthenewgreen)

    Customers start to realize that their “right to be forgotten’” has vanished in a digital world. Every piece of information committed to digital form is subject to instant and inevitable distribution over the internet. The concern is tilting towards frustration in clients’ minds, intensifying their expectation to get in control of their personal data disclosure and usage.

    What is the relevance for banking & insurance?

    • The rising privacy expectations of customers bring important challenges and opportunities for the banks and insurers

    • Will customers give consent to banks in return for receiving personalized offers? How will this affect the ability to provide personalized offers?

    • Protecting and securing customer data: banks and insurers sit atop a horde of digital gold, namely their customers’ personal information.

  • #38 Banking & Insurance - KBC

    All Business is Data Business

    The data-explosion is exponential. Competitors and new entrants across sectors build their businesses around it turning data into the most important driver for profitability. Data has become a key asset: the new oil. A real 360° view is the new standard, making value out of it the differentiator.

    Banks and insurance companies sit on top of piles of data. Less than 10 % is readily accessible and less than 1 % is actually used for analytics: opportunity knocks for product and service improvement. New entrants are taking advantage of the pile of data available ‘out there’ potentially resulting in more insights into customers of traditional banks and insurers. The challenges for bank and insurance companies are two directional:

    OUT-IN: Fear of missing out data. Going from 180° to 360° view. IN-OUT: monetize internal data and contribute in data-sharing ecosystems to create more adding-value for customers

  • #39 Banking & Insurance - KBC

    Internet of Things

    The Internet of Things (IoT) connects multiple devices to the internet which makes these devices accessible from a distance (for instance through mobile apps), and therefore makes it possible to monitor the status, behavior or performance of a property or person. Banks and insurers can use IoT and its data to offer more personalized services, for real-time monitoring of risks and risk prevention.

    Financial institutions will have a more vivid picture of the consumer (towards 360 degree view) allowing them to:

    • Make more accurate lending decisions

    • Deliver more real-time, personalized offers

    • Apply targeted insurance pricing: more risk exposure visibility. For example, connected cars can provide info regarding driving habits.

  • #40 Banking & Insurance - KBC

    Artificial Intelligence

    AI is the ability of a machine to perform cognitive functions associated with human minds, such as reasoning, learning, interacting with the environment and problem solving. Client-centricity: Move closer to clients’ expectations and anticipating them, by studying customer behavior. Create proactive, personalized complete offers. 24/7 client support via chatbots.

    • Cost savings: AI solutions to replace the repetitive cognitive tasks and decision making with machine intelligence in back- and front office.

    • Augmenting decision-making capabilities: building AI-based components enabling faster suggestions to employees, or automated decisions based on risk scoring and event prediction.

    • New workforce: integrating human intelligence with machine intelligence, to coexist in a two-way learning relationship. Will generate adding value via new products, services and/or innovations.

  • #41 Banking & Insurance - KBC

    New Digital Intermediaries

    In the digital world, traditional intermediaries are marginalized. Contrary to expectations, the rise of digital did not only enable direct contact between producers and customers. It also brought new intermediaries operating different business models and disrupting the incumbents as contact with end-customers is at stake. Examples are Uber, Amazone, AirBnb, Spotify.

    • Value is created in keeping the contact with the end customer and bundling products from various suppliers to complete solutions.

    • New digital intermediaries could become the primary customer facing platform. Winning the battle for customers will be crucial. How many digital intermediaries will there be and can financial institutions compete?

    • Will the role of distribution and production be split in the future?

  • #42 Banking & Insurance - KBC

    Quantum Technology

    As modern computers reach the limits of their processing power, quantum computing is starting to offer hope for solving more specialized problems that require immensely robust computing. There are two prerequisites to practical business applications: 1) processors with enough qubits (=quantum bits) to run quantum simulations and 2) quantum algorithms that solve the mathematical problem underlying the application. Processors are still under active development while several algorithms already exist. The result will be enormous processing power and the ability to perform algorithms that normal computers can’t do.

    Quantum computing could create an asymmetry in the market between those who have access to the game changing technology and those who haven’t. As with other advanced technologies the companies that position themselves early to take advantage of quantum computing will establish a significant advantage.
